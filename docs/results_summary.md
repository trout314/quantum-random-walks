# Key Results

## 1. τ Operator Construction

**Result:** The operators τ_a = ±(√7/4) β + (3/4)(e_a · α) satisfy:
- Dirac correspondence: Σ_a e_a^i τ_a = α_i
- Involutory: τ_a² = I (eigenvalues {-1, -1, 1, 1})
- Hermitian: τ_a = τ_a†

**Derivation:** Expanding τ_a in the basis {β, α_1, α_2, α_3} (which all
anticommute pairwise), τ_a² simplifies to (ν² + |s_a|²)I with no cross
terms. The isotropy tensor Σ_a e_a^i e_a^j = (4/3)δ_{ij} forces the spatial
coefficients to s_a = (3/4)e_a, and the constraint Σ_a e_a = 0 kills any
constant offset. The unit norm condition gives ν = ±√7/4.

**Uniqueness:** This is the unique family (up to the ±√7/4 sign) within
span{β, α_1, α_2, α_3}.

Verified symbolically in `src/tau_operators.py` and `tests/test_tau.py`.

## 2. Lattice Structure: Reflections at Each Step

**Result:** When a walker steps through face a of the current tetrahedron,
the directions at the new site are the reflection of the old directions
through the plane perpendicular to e_a:

  R_a(v) = v - 2(v · e_a) e_a

This means:
- The a-th direction at the new site is -e_a (pointing back)
- The other three directions satisfy the coplanarity rule: e_i, e_a, and
  w_i = (2/3)e_a + e_i lie in a common plane
- The new directions form a valid regular tetrahedron (all dot products -1/3)
- Stepping out and back recovers the original tetrahedron (R_a² = I)
- The set of distinct direction orientations is infinite (the rotation angle
  per step is irrational)

Verified in `src/tetrahedron.py` and `tests/test_tetrahedron.py`.

## 3. Position-Dependent τ Operators

**Result:** Since the local directions change at every site, the τ operators
are position-dependent. The τ operators at a new site (after stepping through
face s) relate to the original ones by:

  (e_s · α) τ_a (e_s · α) = -τ'_a

where τ'_a are the τ operators constructed from the new site's directions.
This is an anti-conjugation (note the minus sign), not a conjugation.

**Why the minus sign:** The conjugation by (e_s · α) flips both the β part
(because {α_i, β} = 0) and reflects the α part. The result is -τ'_a rather
than +τ'_a. No simple unitary conjugation U τ_a U† = τ'_a exists because:
- (e_s · α) gives -R on both α and β parts → -τ'
- γ_5 commutes with α_i and anticommutes with β
- No combination resolves both signs simultaneously

## 4. Boerdijk-Coxeter Helix as Walker Path

**Result:** The BC helix is generated by face-stacking tetrahedra. In the
walker model, this corresponds to a repeating period-4 index pattern that
cycles through all 4 directions. The face-exit displacement through face a is
-(2/3)e_a, and directions are reflected through the plane ⊥ to e_a.

**BC helix parameters (for our tetrahedron with edge length √(8/3)):**
- Vertex radius: r = 3√2/5 ≈ 0.849
- Pitch per vertex: h = √(8/3)/√10 ≈ 0.516
- Rotation per vertex: θ = arccos(-2/3) ≈ 131.81° (irrational, so helix never repeats)
- Step size between tet centers: 2/3

**Wiki parametric formula (unit edge length):**
  v_n = (r cos(nθ), r sin(nθ), nh)
  with r = 3√3/10, h = 1/√10, θ = ±arccos(-2/3)

Verified by constructing the helix from face-stacking and matching against
the wiki formula at machine precision.

## 5. BC Helix Pattern and Chirality

**Result:** Starting from the initial tetrahedron {e_0, e_1, e_2, e_3}, the
face-exit index pattern [1, 3, 0, 2] repeating generates a right-handed BC
helix. There are 12 distinct helix axes through any point (24 permutations /
2 since each pattern and its reverse trace the same geometric helix).

**Perpendicular pairs:** The 12 helices form 6 perpendicular pairs. Each pair
consists of one right-handed and one left-handed helix with:
- Perpendicular axes (dot product ≈ 0)
- Non-intersecting axes (skew lines, closest distance ≈ 0.45)
- Overlap only at the starting point

For our original pattern [1,3,0,2] (R), the perpendicular partner is
[0,1,2,3] (L).

## 6. Disjoint Spiral Decomposition

**Result:** All spirals with the same pattern starting from different sites
are disjoint (no shared sites). This was verified numerically: zero overlap
between spirals started from different initial positions.

This enables the walk decomposition W = S_R · S_L, where:
- S_L shifts all walkers along their left-handed spirals simultaneously
- S_R shifts all walkers along their right-handed spirals simultaneously
- Each shift is unitary (collection of independent 1D shifts along
  non-overlapping chains)
- Analogous to the cubic lattice decomposition W = S_z · S_y · S_x

## 7. Frame Transport and Shift Unitarity

**Result:** The shift operator S_R along a BC helix chain is made unitary by
including a frame transport U_{n,n+1} at each step, constructed via the polar
decomposition of the eigenspace overlap matrix. The transport intertwines
the projectors: U P_from^± = P_to^± U, reducing the unitarity condition to
P^+P^- = 0 at a single site.

**Structure:** Each U_{n,n+1} acts as the identity on the +1 eigenspace and
an SU(2) rotation on the -1 eigenspace. The rotation angle is O(1) (not
infinitesimal), determined by the BC helix geometry.

## 8. Conveyor Belt Problem and Coin Resolution

**Problem:** The intertwining frame transport creates a perfect conveyor belt:
eigenstates are spatially uniform, delta functions split but don't spread,
and momentum exp(ikn) has no effect. The transport erases all phase
information between sites.

**Resolution:** A site-local coin operator C_n = exp(-iθ e_{a(n)}·α) applied
before the shift breaks the conveyor belt while preserving unitarity. The
walk W = S·C produces genuine dispersion.

## 9. Massive Dirac Dispersion (1D Helix)

**Result:** On a single BC helix chain with coin angle θ:
- Dispersion relation: **E² = m² + k²** (R² > 0.99)
- Mass gap: **m ≈ 0.59 sin(θ)** (proportional to coin mixing amplitude)
- Speed of light: **c = 1 site/step** (constant, independent of mass)
- Leading edge propagates at c for all masses
- Group velocity decreases with mass: v_g = k/√(k²+m²) < c
- Delta functions spread (genuine dispersion, sigma grows linearly)
- ±E symmetry (particle-antiparticle)
- Exactly unitary for all θ

Verified by: (a) full diagonalization of the walk operator, (b) wavepacket
group velocity measurements, (c) leading edge propagation speed.

## 10. 3D Walk with Coin

**Result (preliminary):** Adding the beta coin to the 3D walk
W = S_R · C · S_L · C gives isotropic spreading with mass-dependent
spreading rate (slower for larger θ). Exactly unitary. But the current
lattice (~80K sites, radius ~22) is too small: the wavepacket fills
it in ~2 steps, preventing clean measurement of the 3D dispersion.

**Infrastructure:** C lattice generator (walk_gen.c) produces isotropic
lattices with balanced R/L coverage using BFS seed + chain expansion +
fill passes. Generates 80K sites with full shift operators in ~2 seconds.
